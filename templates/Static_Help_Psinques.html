By now you have edited your Profile and created various Personas. Now it is time to start establishing contacts or as we call them - psinques.

What is a 'psinque'?

Psinque is our name for a connection between two contacts. However unlike real life connection, a psinque will exist forever, unless consciously severed. In real life, if a peson moves, changes phone number, email address or gets married, chances are that a contact might be lost.

Psinques can divided into incomming, outgoing or both. Let's discuss them in detail and see how they impact the contents of your mobile device's phonebook.

Incomming Psinques

Incomming psinques are all your contacts who share their personal information with you. A, incomming psinque is marked with the following icon.

Outgoing Psinques

Outgoing Psinques are all your contacts that you share your personal information with (as defined within the associated Persona).

Searching and adding contacts

Currently you may only add contacts who already have a psinque account. Searching for contacts requires knowing their primary email address.

  1. In the top menu click Psinques
  2. In the empty field in the New psinque' section type in the primary email of the searched user.
  3. Click on the magnifying glass icon to start the search
  4. If the searched user has enabled public persona, you will be able to immediately add that user to your psinques.
  5. If the searched user has disabled public persona you will have to request private information to add that user to your phone book.

Requesting private information

By default once you add a contact, that contact's public persona will be added to your psinques. If that contact has no public psinque, your contact request will be automatically forwarded as a request to access private data (implicit private information request)

If you already have access too your contact's public persona, you may want to elevate this status in order to access more private data (explicit private information request)

  1. In the top menu click Psinques
  2. Find a contact what you would like to ask to provide you with private information access
  3. Click the key icon next to that contact's personal infomration. The color of the key will change to red to indicate pending request
  4. Once the other party confirms your request, the color will turn into dark grey to indicate that you have been associated with a non-public psinque
